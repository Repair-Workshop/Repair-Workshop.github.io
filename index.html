About the Workshop

Why REPAIR, Why Now

Scientific peer review is under mounting pressure as AI submission volumes surge and large language models reshape how manuscripts are written and evaluated. REPAIR 2026 brings together researchers from machine learning, NLP, information retrieval, HCI, computational social science, scientometrics, and AI governance to study peer review as a technical and institutional system—one that must be measured, supported, and re-designed for the age of AI.

The workshop treats AI in peer review not merely as a technical application area, but as an institutional and epistemic problem. We examine how peer review should evolve when the production, interpretation, and evaluation of scientific knowledge are increasingly mediated by computational systems.

Pillar 01
AI-Mediated Reviewing

LLMs as reviewer assistants, meta-reviewers, or auditors. Automated review generation, summarization, critique, and refinement. Optimal policy for human–AI collaboration in reviewing workflows.

Pillar 02
Measurement, Benchmarks & Evaluation

Principled metrics for review quality: helpfulness, factual grounding, technical accuracy, constructiveness, and consistency. Scalable evaluation infrastructure and benchmark datasets for peer-review research.

Pillar 03
Fairness, Accountability & Trust

Bias detection and mitigation in review assignments and decisions. Transparency and disclosure of AI assistance. Preserving legitimacy and institutional trust in an AI-mediated peer-review ecosystem.

Topics of Interest

We welcome papers on, but not limited to:

  • LLM-assisted and automated review generation
  • AI detection in peer reviews
  • Review quality measurement and benchmarking
  • Factual grounding and claim verification in reviews
  • Reviewer assignment and matching algorithms
  • Bias, fairness, and discrimination in peer review
  • Human–AI collaboration in editorial workflows
  • Consistency, calibration, and variance in reviewing
  • Meta-review and area-chair decision modeling
  • Accountability and transparency of AI-assisted review
  • Datasets and evaluation infrastructure for peer review
  • Policy and governance of AI use in scientific publishing
  • Science of science perspectives on peer-review dynamics
  • Incentive design and strategic behavior in reviewing
