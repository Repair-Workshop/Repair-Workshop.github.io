About the Workshop

Peer review is under increasing pressure as major AI conferences operate at a scale that makes consistent quality, calibration, and fair decision-making difficult to sustain. At the same time, LLMs are reshaping both how manuscripts are produced and how reviews are written. REPAIR 2026 brings together researchers from machine learning, NLP, information retrieval, HCI, computational social science, scientometrics, mechanism design, research integrity, and AI governance to advance the science of peer review in the age of AI.

Theme 01
AI in Manuscript Production

How AI-assisted writing changes the object of review. Questions of originality, authorship, provenance, disclosure, and responsibility when polished expression may obscure conceptual weakness or introduce unverifiable claims.

Theme 02
AI-Mediated Reviewing & Human–AI Collaboration

LLMs as tools for reviewers, area chairs, editors, and program committees. Summarization, critique generation, meta-review support, and decision assistance. Clarifying which forms of AI assistance remain compatible with expert judgment.

Theme 03
Measurement, Benchmarks & Evaluation

Principled metrics for review quality: helpfulness, factual grounding, technical correctness, constructiveness, consistency, and fairness. Scalable evaluation infrastructure and benchmark datasets connected to a theory of what peer review is meant to accomplish.

Theme 04
Fairness, Accountability & Governance

Disclosure policies, bias, unequal access to AI tools, manipulation risks, and conference governance. Conditions under which AI-assisted reviewing can remain trustworthy, legitimate, and oriented toward the integrity of the research record.

Submissions

Call for Papers

We invite full papers, short papers, extended abstracts, demos, position papers, negative results, and early-stage work on all aspects of peer review in the age of AI. We particularly encourage submissions that connect technical methods to real peer-review workflows, deployed systems, conference policy, or empirical evidence from scientific communities. All accepted submissions will be non-archival and managed via OpenReview. Each submission receives 3 reviews.

Topics of Interest

We welcome papers on, but not limited to:

  • LLM-assisted and automated review generation
  • AI detection in peer reviews
  • Review quality measurement and benchmarking
  • Factual grounding and claim verification in reviews
  • Reviewer assignment and matching algorithms
  • Bias, fairness, and discrimination in peer review
  • Human–AI collaboration in editorial workflows
  • Consistency, calibration, and variance in reviewing
  • Meta-review and area-chair decision modeling
  • Accountability and transparency of AI-assisted review
  • Datasets and evaluation infrastructure for peer review
  • Policy and governance of AI use in scientific publishing
  • Science of science perspectives on peer-review dynamics
  • Incentive design and strategic behavior in reviewing
